CoMoSpeech: One-Step Speech and Singing Voice Synthesis via Consistency Model


Abstract

Recent progress in large-scale speech generation has been remarkably advanced by the language model and diffusion model. However, the generation process of both methods is slow and computationally intensive. Real-time speech generation using a lower computing budget to achieve quality on par with previous work remains a significant challenge. In this paper, we present VoiceLCM, a large-scale high-quality speech generation system achieving similar quality with lower that 10% of the computational cost compared with previous work. VoiceLCM is built on the latent consistency model and applies a novel adversarial consistency training approach that can train from scratch without the need for a pre-trained teacher model. Furthermore, we also propose a novel prosody generation module that enhances the diversity of prosody, making the rhythm of the speech sound more natural. The generation processes of VoiceLCM can be achieved efficiently with one or two sampling steps while maintaining high audio quality and high similarity with the prompt in zero-shot speech generation. Our experimental results show the superior performance of VoiceLCM. In particular, voiceLCM can achieve more than 15x faster than other zero-shot TTS systems with comparable performance in terms of voice quality, similarity, and prosody.
